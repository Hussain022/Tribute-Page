Nelson Mendela

The revered anti-apartheid activist and South African president, fought for equality and reconciliation, leaving an enduring legacy of peace and social justice.

Here's a time line of Nelson Mandela's life:

  • 1918 - Born in the village of Mvezo, Transkei, South Africa
  • 1941 - Joins the African National Congress (ANC)
  • 1952 - Leads the Defiance Campaign against apartheid laws
  • 1956 - Arrested and charged with high treason along with 155 others; later acquitted
  • 1962 - Arrested and sentenced to five years in prison for incitement and leaving the country without a passport
  • 1964 - Sentenced to life in prison for sabotage during the Rivonia Trial
  • 1990 - Released from prison after 27 years
  • 1993 - Wins Nobel Peace Prize along with South African President F.W. de Klerk
  • 1994 - Elected as South Africa's first black president in the country's first multiracial elections
  • 1999 - Steps down as president after one term in office
  • 2001 - Diagnosed and treated for prostate cancer
  • 2013 - Dies at the age of 95 in Johannesburg, South Africa
"Nelson Mandela's life story is a testament to the power of perseverance, forgiveness, and reconciliation in the face of oppression and injustice."

-- President Barack Obama
